# 🚀 Bye-Bye Linux Passwords! 🐧💻

Are you a Linux user tired of typing your password every single time you open your laptop or run `sudo`? 😩

I just found a way to solve this! Now, I open my laptop and authenticate just by looking at my camera—Windows Hello style, but better because it's Linux! 🛡️📷

### 🔍 How it works:
I used **Howdy**, a powerful open-source library that brings facial authentication to Linux using **OpenCV** and **dlib** for facial recognition. It integrates directly into the PAM (Pluggable Authentication Modules) stack, so it works everywhere: logging in, unlocking the screen, and even `sudo` in the terminal!

### 🛠️ Tech Stack:
- **Howdy**: The core facial recognition engine.
- **dlib**: For high-accuracy face identification models.
- **OpenCV**: To handle the camera stream.
- **PAM Integration**: To hook into the system's authentication layer.

### 🐧 Compatibility:
Tested on **Kali Linux 2025** (Debian-based), but works on most distributions like Ubuntu, Arch, and Fedora.
